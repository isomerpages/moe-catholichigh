<h3>English &amp; English Literature</h3>
<p>Our school believes in the infinite potential of every child to love and
enjoy the English language:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>We conduct drama lessons in&nbsp;the curriculum&nbsp;also at the lower
levels to give budding thespians a chance to express themselves.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>At the upper levels, we have projects like Instapoetry that combine social
media and literature appreciation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Theatre outing once a year for Upper Levels for out-of-classroom literary
experiences.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Our students do real-world projects such as writing a policy paper and
designing a school curriculum.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><em>“The English department teachers in Catholic High are very inspiring and have on multiple occasions prompted me to think above and beyond what is in the syllabus, and applying the skills I learned to analysing and understanding the real world.”</em>
</p>
<p><em>– Gideon Law (4-3/2016)</em>
</p>

<h3>Our Approach</h3>
<p><strong>Overarching Approach and Purpose</strong>
</p>
<p>Using a diverse range of texts, contexts and platforms to explore the
human condition through:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Fundamentals in Linguistics, Logic and Literature</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Broad Exposure and Experiences</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Deep Exploration of Individual Talent Areas.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>To equip and enable our students with the ability to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Make sense, construct meaning and effect change in a fast dynamic world.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Exercise literary sensitivities and inculcate nuanced perspectives.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To be passionate, articulate and empathetic gentlemen of principles and
convictions.</p>
</li>
</ul>
